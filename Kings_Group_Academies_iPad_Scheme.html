Everything you need to know

Your questions answered

Common questions from families about the King's Group Academies 1:1 iPad Scheme.

The iPad is the most intuitive device on the market and offers over 500,000 educational applications - unmatched by any competitor. The 11-inch screen with 128GB of storage gives students ample space and display for all curriculum activities, from note-taking and research to creative projects and video production.
Digital skills are now a basic expectation in almost every career. Research confirms that good home access to technology positively impacts educational achievement - it motivates students, improves the quality and presentation of their work, and allows them to explore subjects in their own time.
The device remains the property of King's Group Academies throughout your chosen payment term. Ownership transfers to your family once all agreed contributions have been completed - whether you chose the upfront option or a 12, 24 or 36-month plan. It is then yours to keep.
The scheme includes 3-year accidental damage cover with no excess, provided the device is kept in the supplied Deqster Smart Rugged Touch Plus keyboard case. If the device needs repair or replacement, contact the school in the first instance and we will guide you through the claims process.
No child will be excluded from the scheme because of financial circumstances. Please come and speak to us in confidence so we can find a way to ensure your child is included. Our commitment is that every student has access to the same learning opportunities.
We strongly encourage all families to participate. If you opt out, your child will not be provided with a personal device to take home. Students will still have access to shared devices in school, but they will not have their own iPad for home use.
Yes, provided it meets our minimum specification - a current-generation iPad with stylus support and sufficient storage for educational apps. The device will need to be wiped and our Jamf device management software installed, in line with Government and DfE guidance. Please contact us before assuming an existing device is compatible.
All iPads are managed through Jamf, which restricts students to school-approved applications on the school network. Internet access is filtered through Smoothwall, which blocks inappropriate content and is updated regularly as the internet evolves. Students are also asked to install an HTTPS certificate to support their online safety. Parents can additionally monitor usage at home through Jamf's free parental controls.
We ask all students to keep their iPad out of sight in their bag whilst travelling to and from school. iPads can be locked, tracked, located if lost, and have data removed remotely. In the unlikely event that a child is approached and asked to hand over their device, they should do so without resistance and inform you immediately so you can contact the Police and the school.
The scheme is a financial commitment entered into on behalf of the whole cohort. Withdrawing midway through would affect the school's finances and the scheme's sustainability for all families. We ask all participants to treat this as a firm commitment for the duration of the chosen term.
Students submit work through Showbie, Socrative and Explain Everything - all pre-installed on the device. Teachers can view, annotate and mark work directly through these platforms, and students receive feedback in the same way.
Ideally, every student should have access to the internet at home, but no additional equipment is required. Students may choose to purchase apps, though there are thousands of free apps available. We will never ask you to purchase apps directly - the school selects and pre-installs everything needed.
